# Demand Survey — Concert Ticket Re-release Alerts

Target respondents: people who have tried to buy tickets for a popular concert in
the past 12 months. Goal: quantify the pain and the willingness to pay (Component 2).

## Screening
1. In the last 12 months, did you try to buy tickets for a concert/show that sold out? (Yes / No — end if No)
2. How many such events did you try for? (1 / 2–3 / 4+)

## The pain (time & effort)
3. For your most recent attempt, did you get the tickets you wanted? (Yes / Partly / No)
4. Roughly how long did you actively spend refreshing/queuing/searching? (<15 min / 15–60 min / 1–3 h / >3 h)
5. Did you keep checking for refunds or re-released tickets after the initial sale? (Yes, manually / Yes, with a tool / No)
6. How stressful was the experience? (1 = not at all … 5 = extremely)

## Current workarounds
7. What did you use to try to catch re-released tickets? (Refreshing the site / Resale groups / A bot or alert service / Other)
8. Have you ever bought from a scalper / above face value? (Yes / No / Considered it)

## Willingness to pay
9. If a service pushed you an instant alert the moment official tickets were re-released, how valuable would that be? (1–5)
10. Would you pay for such a service? (No / Only if free / Yes)
11. If yes, what monthly price feels fair? (NT$0 / 1–49 / 50–99 / 100–199 / 200+)
12. Or would you prefer to pay per event instead of monthly? (Per-event NT$___ / Prefer monthly / Either)

## Open-ended
13. What is the single most frustrating part of buying popular tickets today?
14. Anything that would make you trust (or distrust) an alert service like this?
